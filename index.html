    Threads of Resilience

    Rebuilding after difficult seasons with needles, wool, and the light of the Glarus Alps.

    05 Feb 2024 · 6 min read

    When a sudden diagnosis unsettled my family, knitting became my breathing space and the way I measured hope.

    I began each dawn by casting on ten slow rows, syncing breath to movement while the valley woke outside. Recording the texture of each yarn kept my thoughts present and softened anxious spirals.

    Resilience rows I rely on

    • Sync breath to stitches: inhale on knit, exhale on purl.
    • Pair every new yarn with a journal page capturing the memory it holds.
    • Take a midday walk in the valley before choosing the next colour.
    • Share a weekly progress snapshot with friends to celebrate small loops.

    These rituals turned projects into gentle anchors. Colours drawn from lake reflections and larch bark reminded me that change is seasonal, not final.

    “Knitting teaches me that progress is quiet; loop by loop we return to ourselves.”

    Now I invite neighbours and learners to join virtual knit nights from Glarus, weaving our stories together so no one feels alone with their needles.

    Comforting materials

    Current project

    Handspun Swiss merino held with baby alpaca on 4.5 mm wooden needles, palette inspired by the dawn over Tödi.

    I sip chamomile and lemon-balm tea while soft jazz keeps the rhythm steady and shoulders relaxed.

  • Care Guide

    Wool & Wellness Rituals

    Gentle maintenance routines that care for fibers and for the hands that craft them.

    22 Jan 2024 · 5 min read

    Caring for wool is an act of self-kindness; when I slow down with warm water and soap, my breathing follows.

    Prepare a mindful soak

    Fill a basin with lukewarm spring water, stir in a teaspoon of lanolin-rich soap, and let the fragrance of mountain pine rise before immersing the garment.

    Create a restorative finish

    Press fibres between towels with open palms, stretch gently on blocking mats, and allow your shoulders to drop as the piece relaxes.

    Wellness checklist

    • Light a beeswax candle to mark the care session as special.
    • Add a drop of lavender or pine for alpine calm.
    • Massage wrists and fingers while easing water from the wool.
    • Journal a sensory note once the piece is drying flat.

    These pauses keep garments luminous and remind me that maintenance is a space to listen inward, not a chore to rush.

    Aromatherapy palette

    • Cedar soaking board for grounding scent.
    • Eco wool wash with chamomile extract.
    • Microfiber towels reserved for knitwear only.
    • Blocking wires wrapped in linen tape for gentle hold.

    Weekly rhythm

    Alternate deep cleans with outdoor airing so fibres rest; give yourself the same alternating rhythm between effort and recovery.

    Contemplating Alpine Horizons

    A meditative walk through Glarus landscapes that colour my shawls and soothe the heart.

    10 Dec 2023 · 4 min read

    Some designs begin with silence. I board the train toward Linthal, sketching peaks as sunrise spills over the valley.

    Colour notes from the valley

    Frosted meadows offer silvery sage, while the Linth River gifts deep teal. I capture each hue in a palette notebook before it fades.

    Observation prompts

    Pause, name three colours you see, note how the air feels on your skin, and imagine which stitch texture could hold that feeling.

    Mindful travel companions

    A lightweight camera, an audio recorder of cowbells, and a pouch of swatches travel with me so ideas return to the studio unhurried.

    By the time I knit the first rows, the landscape lives inside the fabric, ready to comfort anyone who wraps it around their shoulders.

    Glarus field notes

    • Linth River overlook – teal & slate for gradient socks.
    • Klöntal pine forest – moss green with copper flecks.
    • Schwammhöhe ridge – rosy dusk for a lace border.
    • Niederurnen orchards – soft apricot for mittens.

    Next outing

    Planning a sunrise sketch session at Lake Klöntal with thermos chai and a basket of fresh swatches.

    Inside the Chalet Studio

    A look at the routines that keep my Glarus retirement atelier vibrant and welcoming.

    18 Nov 2023 · 5 min read

    The studio wakes at 6 a.m. when I open the shutters to watch pink light brush the Tödi peak.

    I warm the space with a small wood stove, then line up yarn cakes chosen the night before. A gratitude note sits beside the swift.

    Studio rhythms

    • 06:30 kettle + gratitude journaling.
    • 07:15 colour planning under north-facing window.
    • 09:00 dye pot check-in and stretch break.
    • 16:00 tidy surfaces, photograph progress, close the day.

    Retirement means I can move slowly; I listen to the weather and let it decide whether I dye, design, or simply mend.

    This rhythm keeps my mind clear and reminds me that craft is a companion, not a race.

    Studio essentials

    • Maple worktable built by my father.
    • Vintage swift rescued from a Basel flea market.
    • Set of 3.75–5 mm birch needles for everyday projects.
    • Playlist of Swiss jazz and the sound of snowmelt.

    Community note

    I host a monthly knit circle at Gemeindehausplatz; newcomers receive tea, pattern notes, and a hug at the door.
